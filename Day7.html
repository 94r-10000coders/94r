CHAPTER - 1

Introduction:

Hydrogen is the simplest element of all, and the lightest. It is also by far the most common element in the Universe. Over 90 percent of the atoms in the Universe are hydrogen. In its commonest form, the hydrogen atom is made of one proton, one electron, and no neutrons. Hydrogen is the only element that can exist without neutrons. On Earth, the major location of hydrogen is in water, H2O.

CHAPTER - 2

Uses Of Hydrogen:

Large quantities of hydrogen are used in the Haber process (production of ammonia),hydrogenation of fats and oils, methanol production, hydrocracking, and hydrodesulfurization. Hydrogen is also used in metal refining.Compressed hydrogen is used as a fuel for fuel cell vehicles, which generate electricity through a chemical reaction between hydrogen and oxygen.

CHAPTER - 3

  1. Compressed HYdrogen:

    2. hydrogen gas is compressed to high pressures of up to 700 bar (10,000 psi) and stored in high-pressure tanks.

  2. Liquid Hydrogen:

    3. Gaseous hydrogen is liquefied by cooling it to below −253°C (−423°F). Once hydrogen is liquefied it can be stored at the liquefaction plant in large insulated tanks at -162°C.

  3. Metal Hydride:

    4. hydrogen is stored in a solid state by using metal alloys that can absorb hydrogen and form metal hydride. When the metal hydride is heated, the hydrogen gas is released and can be used as a fuel source.
